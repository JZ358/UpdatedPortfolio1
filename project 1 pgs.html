Wood Pavilion

Adaptive Re-Use

Silo Renewal

Ten spherical spaces of three different sizes are the spatial platforms dedicated to gallery and exhibition areas with diverse purposes. Offices, storage spaces and washrooms are located in the central buildings for ease of access and transportation of art pieces. Silos on the ground floor are cut out to create larger floor space for the main lobby, dining areas and flexible exhibition and multi-usage zones.

Program & Circulation

Interior Design

Spherical program spaces are distributed across the silo, which act as the compression force pressing against the silos. Simulated with sponge-like characteristics, the silos would take on the shape of the sphere which forms the negative spaces for circulation. These negative spaces allow for transitions between enclosed silos interiors and open platforms.

Roof Top Garden

& Restaurant

Slits that mimic the cutting and stretching of fabric are used to create horizontal and vertical glass openings that extend across different floors. Echoing the sponge-like simulations performed on the interior silos, the exterior facade uses concrete to create soft-body qualities of fabric.

As the design developed, the slit opening and roof-top designs unintentionally reflected characteristics of yonic architecture. This accidental gesture elicited further discussions and thoughts on portrayals of feminism and human anatomy. The relationship between form and materiality where softness and rigidness collide could also have symbolic associations with the gentle but powerful qualities of feminine strength.

Parametric Design:

Twisting Tower

Concept

This parametric design investigates form in developing its structural and facade system. Its exterior configuration, floor plate patterning in addition to the twisted facade all demonstrate an exploration of unique, organic shapes.

The three-parts composition portrays the collision between free-flowing forms and rigid structures, transitioning from an organic, star-shaped base wraped in a lattice structure, which is partially concealed with smooth ribbon-like facade twisted around the tower.
